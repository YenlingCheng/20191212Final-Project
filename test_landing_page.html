Introduction

What's Fake News

Fake news is a form of news consisting of deliberate disinformation or hoaxes spread via traditional news media or online social media.

Fake news is written and published usually with the intent to mislead or control information or to shape domestic political attitude.

Fake news often makes use of sensational, dishonest, or outright fabricated headlines to increase readership.

How is Fake News spread

Fake news is spread through social media to reach receptive audiences.

Twitter and Facebook are the most prominent platforms for social media manipulation.The use of ‘bots’ amplify manipulated contents.

Cookies are used to track people's visit to websites and thus create personality profiles, and show people fake news content that they are most receptive to.

How to cope with it

Embed media literacy into school curriculum

Read the entire piece before you decide whether or not to share.

Don't stay in your own echo chamber

Use Factcheck tools such as Factcheck.org
